Financial Foundations for Thriving Communities

5 Pillars
of Financial
Success

Created by GFOA, the Financial Foundations Framework helps facilitate collaboration and support for public policies and programs. Organized into five core pillars, the Framework offers strategies for developing stronger foundations and more cooperative communities to ensure long-term financial health.

Pillar 1

Establish A Long-term Vision

Give people a reason to cooperate.

Leadership Strategies #1:
Promote Collaboration

The only thing better than an inspiring vision is an inspiring shared vision. Turn stakeholders into co-creators as you define the long-term vision.

Leadership Strategies #2:
Balance Long-Term Goals with Short-Term Needs

In all things, seek balance. Advocate for both the big picture and day-to-day needs. Break long-term goals in shorter-term milestones to maintain momentum.

Pillar 2

Build Trust And Open Communication

Create the conditions for cooperation.

Leadership Strategies #3:
Create open lines of communication.

Communication is key. Create and promote two-way channels to connect elected officials and staff with the public.

Leadership Strategies #4:
Cultivate trustworthy reputations.

When it comes to financial sustainability, trust is everything. Build trust through transparency—say what you mean, mean what you say and follow through.

Pillar 3

Use Collective Decision Making

Leadership Strategies #5:
Engage key stakeholders.

Financial sustainability is a team effort. As leaders, we must be proactive and responsive. Let people voice their concerns. Ask for their feedback. Adjust as needed.

Institutional Design Principles #1: Collective Choice Arrangements.

Give citizens a seat at the table. They’re more likely to support decisions when they have a say in how public resources are used. Prioritize public feedback.

Institutional Design Principles #2: Networked Enterprises.

We’re stronger together. Share services across local governments to save costs. Build collaborative, cross-sector networks to pool resources for addressing community challenges.

Pillar 4

Create Clear Rules

Create the conditions for cooperation.

Institutional Design Principles #3:
Well-Defined Boundaries

When the game has clear rules, everyone’s set up to win. Create and share expectations about how decisions get made, who has the final say, and timelines.

Institutional Design Principles #4:
Monitoring

Financial sustainability relies on everyone following the rules. Hold people accountable. When they understand their reputations are at stake, they’re more likely to play fair.

Leadership Strategies #6:
Maintain Oversight

Cooperation is key to financial sustainability. Reinforce the importance of cooperation through the power of the purse strings. Encourage a culture of shared values.

Pillar 5

Treat Everyone Fairly

Create the conditions for cooperation.

Institutional Design Principles #6:
Proportional Equivalence Between Benefits & Cost

People must get what they pay for and pay for what they get. Be proactive— make sure citizens know what they stand to gain for their tax dollar.

Institutional Design Principles #7:
Conflict-Resolution Mechanisms

We can’t please everyone all the time. Disputes around financial decisions will happen. Anticipate that. Prevent destructive conflict. Promote constructive debate.

Institutional Design Principles #8:
Minimum Recognition of Rights

Local governments must have autonomy. Use collective decision-making to develop policies and procedures for protecting local choices and addressing unfunded mandates and grants.

Why it matters:
Immediate Impact,
Long-Term Leadership

Strong financial foundations create the kind of cities that are built to last, places we want to live, communities where all people contribute to the common good and are cared for in return. This framework is a roadmap. By following it, we can make an immediate difference in how government business gets done, while ensuring our communities' well-being for generations to come.
